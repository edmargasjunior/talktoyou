TalkToYou Help and User Manual

This manual explains how to use TalkToYou, an alternative and augmentative communication application designed to help people express themselves through cards with images, text, sounds and routines.

Application goal: to support daily communication by allowing the person to tap cards to express needs, feelings, choices, discomfort, routines and important situations.
Academic project: TalkToYou is a technical-scientific product developed by Edmar Geraldo Almeida de Souza Junior as part of the Master's program at the Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG), 2026.

1. Home screen

When opening the application, the user sees the main board with categories such as:

  • I Want: immediate needs and requests;
  • Communication: quick answers such as yes, no, help and stop;
  • How I Feel: feelings, pain, hunger, thirst and discomfort;
  • Food: solid foods;
  • Drink: liquids;
  • Routine: daily activities;
  • Sensory: noise, light, discomfort and breaks;
  • Emergency: important or urgent situations;
  • People: family members, teachers, therapists and friends;
  • Play: toys, leisure and interests.

2. What cards and folders are

TalkToYou uses two main types of items:

Card

A card represents a word, action, feeling, object or need. When tapped, the application speaks the text or plays the recorded audio associated with that card.

Examples:

  • Water
  • I am in pain
  • Yes
  • I do not want
  • Loud noise

Folder

A folder is a category that contains other cards.

Examples:

  • I Want
  • Food
  • Routine
  • Sensory

3. How to use the board

Step 1: tap a main folder, such as I Want.
Step 2: tap a card inside it, such as Water.
Step 3: the application will speak or play the corresponding sound.

To return to the previous screen, use the back arrow at the top of the app or the Android system back button.

4. How sentence composition works

Some folders can combine their name with the selected card. This allows the app to generate simple functional phrases.

Example:

  • Folder: I Want
  • Card: Water
  • Spoken sentence: I want water

Other folders are used only for organization. In these cases, the app should speak only the selected card.

Example:

  • Folder: Sensory
  • Card: Loud noise
  • Spoken sentence: Loud noise

When creating or editing a folder, the caregiver can enable or disable the option: “Combine this folder name with the selected card”.

5. How to create a new card

Step 1: tap the menu button ☰.
Step 2: choose Add New.
Step 3: choose whether the item will be a Card or a Folder.
Step 4: choose where it will be placed using the Parent field.
Step 5: enter the card or folder name.
Step 6: choose an image, record audio if desired, and tap Save on device.

6. How to edit or delete cards

Step 1: tap the menu button ☰.
Step 2: choose Manage Items.
Step 3: tap Edit on the desired item.
Step 4: update the information and save, or tap Delete.
When deleting a folder, the cards inside it may also be removed. Use this option carefully.

7. How to add an image

When creating or editing a card, tap Take or Choose Photo.

Depending on the device, the user may choose an image from the gallery or take a new photo using the camera.

Using real images from the person's daily life can improve recognition and functional use, especially for children and people who benefit from concrete visual references.

8. How to record custom audio

TalkToYou allows caregivers or users to record a custom voice for each card.

Step 1: open the card creation or editing form.
Step 2: tap the microphone button 🎤.
Step 3: speak the desired message.
Step 4: tap again to stop recording.
Step 5: save the card.

If a card has recorded audio, the application uses that audio. Otherwise, it uses the device's speech synthesis voice.

9. How to use the alarm

Each card can have an optional alarm time in HH:mm format (for example, 07:30).

Step 1: when creating or editing a card, fill in Alarm (Optional).
Step 2: save the card. The time is stored on the device.
Step 3: keep TalkToYou open or installed as an app on the phone/tablet so alarms can be checked.

You can set multiple daily alarms on different cards (breakfast, medicine, school, bath, sleep, etc.). If two or more cards share the same time, the app triggers alerts one after another in sequence.

When the time arrives:

  • a full-screen alarm appears with the card image;
  • the app speaks a voice alert (for example, “Time to…” + card name), using recorded audio or device speech synthesis;
  • tap OK to dismiss the current alarm and move to the next one in the same minute, if any.
Tip: use the Routine folder with real photos to reinforce what each activity means.

10. How to choose the board voice

In the menu ☰, use the Board Voice option.

Available voices depend on the device, operating system, browser and installed languages.

When the app language is changed, TalkToYou tries to prioritize voices compatible with the selected language.

11. How to change the app language

TalkToYou can detect the smartphone language automatically. However, the caregiver can manually choose another language in the app menu.

Step 1: open the menu ☰.
Step 2: find App Language.
Step 3: choose Portuguese, English or Spanish.

The interface updates without requiring the user to clear app data.

Important: custom cards created by the user are not translated automatically. This protects personal names, routines, therapy phrases and family-specific vocabulary. Only official system cards may be translated by the app.

12. How to change card size

In the menu ☰, use the Card Size option.

Available options include:

  • Automatic;
  • Large — useful for low vision or young children;
  • Medium;
  • Small;
  • Mini.

13. Backup and restore (secure)

TalkToYou stores everything on the device (IndexedDB). To avoid losing cards, custom photos and voice recordings, use JSON backup from the ☰ menu.

Export backup

Open the menu ☰ and choose Export Backup.

The app creates a Backup_TalkToYou_YYYY-MM-DD.json file in TalkToYou v3 format, including:

  • folders and cards (names, hierarchy, alarms, sentence mode);
  • base64 images;
  • recorded audio encoded safely in the file;
  • app version metadata (useful for support and research).

Save the file to cloud storage, e-mail or USB. No data is sent to external servers.

Import backup

Step 1: export a backup of current data before importing another file.
Step 2: in the ☰ menu, choose Import Backup and select only JSON files exported by TalkToYou.
Step 3: confirm replacement when prompted.
Import replaces the current board. Oversized, corrupted or non-v3 files are rejected. If import fails, TalkToYou attempts to automatically restore the data that existed before.

14. Learn module

Learn is a guided activity to practice communication using the same cards, images and audio as your board — useful at home, school or therapy.

Step 1: in the ☰ menu, enable Enable Learning. A Learn card appears on the board home screen.
Step 2: tap Learn, choose the category (folder) to practice, then tap Start Game.
Step 3: cards appear on screen (target + choices), then the app plays the spoken instruction — see and hear at the same time.
Step 4: tap cards in the correct order. Each tap plays recorded audio or device voice. In sentence composition folders, order may be folder “I Want” + card “Water”.
Step 5: when correct, you hear success feedback and return to category selection to repeat or choose another folder.

Wrong sequences prompt retry without leaving the round. Real photos and recorded audio strengthen image–sound–meaning links.

Note: Learn only reads your board; it does not change cards. Disable it in the menu when not in use.

15. Clearing local app data

The app includes an option to clear local data from the device.

This may remove:

  • cards created on the device;
  • images added to cards;
  • recorded audio;
  • voice and layout preferences;
  • other local settings.
Before clearing data, export a backup. This allows the board to be restored later if needed.

16. Print or generate PDF

In the menu ☰, tap Print Board.

This function generates a PDF version of the board. It can be printed, laminated or used as a physical communication support.

17. Therapeutic organization suggestion

A useful communication board should prioritize functional communication.

Instead of thinking only about categories, consider the real needs of the person:

  • What does the person need to request?
  • What does the person need to refuse?
  • How can the person communicate pain or discomfort?
  • How can the person ask for help?
  • How can the person express preferences?
  • How can the person participate in daily routines?

It is recommended to personalize cards with real images of the home, family members, objects, foods, places and activities that are part of the person's life.

TalkToYou can support different contexts, including autism, temporary speech difficulties after accidents or illness, and educational activities such as vocabulary learning in another language.

18. Common problems

The audio did not play

  • Check if the device volume is enabled.
  • Try selecting another voice in the menu.
  • Some browsers block audio until the first user tap.

The microphone did not record

  • Check whether microphone permission was granted.
  • Try using the installed app version instead of a browser tab.
  • Some browsers restrict recording when the app is not running in a secure context.

My cards disappeared

  • Check whether app or browser data was cleared.
  • Restore a backup if available.
  • Avoid clearing app data before exporting a backup.

The app opened with a browser bar at the top

  • This may happen if the app is opened as a browser page instead of a trusted installed app.
  • It may also be related to PWA/TWA or Android asset links configuration.
  • The HTML page itself cannot hide browser security bars.
Tip: before making major changes to the board, export a backup. This helps prevent loss of personalized content.
